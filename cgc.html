What is the CGC Programme?

The Canine Good Citizen (CGC) is a national certification programme administered by Dogs New Zealand. It is designed for everyday dog owners and their pets, testing dogs in simulated everyday situations in a relaxed atmosphere.

The CGC programme emphasises practical skills and good manners rather than precision performance. As the programme states: assessment “does NOT require that you and your dog perform with precision” — the focus is on demonstrating reliable, well-mannered behaviour.

Eligibility

  • Dogs must be over 12 months old
  • Both purebred and crossbred dogs are welcome
  • Dogs NZ membership is not required

Skills Assessed

  • Appearance and grooming
  • Food manners
  • Accepting strangers
  • Controlled walking (on lead)
  • Staying tied up
  • Behaviour around other dogs

Certification Levels

Level Description
CGC Foundation level — entry-level good citizen assessment
Bronze Intermediate level with expanded skills
Silver Advanced skills and reliability
Gold Highest level of Canine Good Citizen achievement

Costs & Registration

Individual clubs set their own fees for training and assessments. Dogs that pass are registered in the Dogs NZ national database and receive certificates.
